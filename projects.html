RNG Hell Series

RNG Hell

Fall 2019

The original RNG Hell was the first video game I ever developed, with it being used as the final project of my Programming I course at John Abbott College, which was taught by Larry Fagen. When developing this first project, I tried to keep my scope for the game as small as possible and instead provide an experience that isn't commonly seen in games in order to produce something unique without having to implement mechanics which were out of my reach at the time. After coming up with multiple mold-breaking ideas, I wound up creating a game that is highly dependent on luck, with most deaths being caused by random instant kills. For good measure, I even implemented a feature which can delete the player's score if they happen to be unlucky, which can trigger even during what would otherwise be successful runs! Needless to say this is not a game that is meant to be taken seriously, unless you have a gambling addiction and an interest in destroying your computer.

RNG Hell Online

Fall 2020

This sequel to the original RNG Hell was developed as the final project for my Web Programming I course, which was taught by Helen Katalifos. For this second attempt, I aimed to showcase how much I had improved over the past year by developing a new RNG Hell game which completely surpassed the original, while still retaining the frantic gameplay that is endemic to the franchise. This was accomplished through the introduction of multiple new features, such as two new attacks, the ability to fire melee attacks in 4 different directions, a new luck stat which reduces the odds of randomly getting instantly killed (along with the chances of having one's score deleted), and an all-new Online mode. This Online mode features the "latest and greatest" netcode, which will frequently slow down gameplay, allow enemies to occasionally "rollback" to life, and randomly disconnect; prematurely ending the player's game without a score to show for it, all without requiring an actual internet connection!

Steel Hostilities

Fall 2021

Steel Hostilities is a Sports/Fighting game that is heavily inspired by the Punch-Out!! series which tasks players to defeat three bosses as a warrior armed with light punches, heavy sword slashes, the ability to dodge horizontally, and two super moves which can quickly turn the tide of battle. The campaign does not end upon the defeat of the final boss, as skilled fighters can endlessly rechallenge stronger versions of all three opponents in pursuit of a high score. Should a combatant find themself exhausted of constantly having to fight, Steel Hostilities includes an automatic "quick-save" feature which will allow the contender to take a break and return to their most recent fight, though this can only be done once per bout to prevent save scumming. The game was developed by Ryan Bujold and I as the final project for a Game Programming course at John Abbott College, which was taught by Vikram Singh.
