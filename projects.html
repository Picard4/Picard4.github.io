Steel Hostilities

Fall 2021

Steel Hostilities is a Sports/Fighting game that is heavily inspired by the Punch-Out!! series which tasks players to defeat three bosses as a warrior armed with light punches, heavy sword slashes, the ability to dodge horizontally, and two super moves which can quickly turn the tide of battle. The campaign does not end upon the defeat of the final boss, as skilled fighters can endlessly rechallenge stronger versions of all three opponents in pursuit of a high score. Should a combatant find themself exhausted of constantly having to fight, Steel Hostilities includes an automatic "quick-save" feature which will allow the contender to take a break and return to their most recent fight, though this can only be done once per bout to prevent save scumming. The game was developed by Ryan Bujold and I as the final project for a Game Programming course at John Abbott College, which was taught by Vikram Singh in the Fall of 2021.

Designing for Multiple Systems

When it comes to collecting video games, I often enjoy buying multiple different versions of the same game to compare the differences. On top of that, one of my favourite consoles of all time is the Nintendo Wii U, a device that is infamous for its lack of third party support. Due to the way I like to enjoy my video games, I've always been curious about the needs of different platforms when running the same program. Currently I've only learned about developing for PC, with the exception of web development, so in time I hope to learn what makes certain machines more or less desirable for developers when compared to the common customer.
